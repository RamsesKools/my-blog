# Interstellar (2014)

**Rating: 9/10**

Christopher Nolan's sci-fi epic about astronauts searching for a new home for humanity.

<!-- more -->

Three things stand out:

- **Music.** Hans Zimmer's score is one of his best — the organ-driven tracks during the docking scene and the wormhole sequence are genuinely breathtaking.
- **Visuals.** The black hole (Gargantua) was rendered using actual physics simulations and produced a paper-worthy result. It's still the most accurate on-screen depiction of a black hole.
- **Acting.** Matthew McConaughey carries the emotional weight of the film throughout.

The one point I dock is for the ending. The "love transcending time and space" framing and the black hole interior sequence push a bit too far into the metaphysical for my taste — the film is at its best when it stays grounded in the hard science fiction.

Still a near-perfect film and one I keep coming back to.
